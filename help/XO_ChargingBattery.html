Charging the Battery

Your XO needs power to run. Power is electricity, and your XO can get it in two ways:

  • from its battery.
  • from a power cord plugged into a power source.

Using battery power

The XO battery is a storage container for electricity. When you first get your XO, this container is empty, so you need to fill it up. That means you have to store some electricity in your battery before you can travel away from the power cord.

This is the XO battery outside of the case. The battery goes on the back of your XO.

Installing the battery

  1. Line up the four tabs on the new battery with the long edge furthest from the catches.
  2. Drop the battery into place.
  3. Slide the left-hand side catch to the left, and press the battery gently into place.
  4. Slide the right-hand side catch to the left, to lock the battery in place.

Charging the battery

  1. Ensure that the battery is in the back of the XO's case.
  2. Plug the power cord into your XO.
  3. Plug the other end of the power cord into the nearest power source, such as the one connected to the school's generator or a 12 Volt car battery.

Plugging in your XO

You can use your XO while it is plugged in, when you are charging the battery or when you have a good source for power. For power sources, you can use the generator supplied by your school, the electrical grid, or another source of electricity.

When to charge the battery

The battery icon in the Frame fills with color, showing you the current charge, or amount of power in the battery. Move the pointer over the battery icon to see how full the battery is. While the battery charges, the battery light near the power button (shaped like a battery) should display yellow, turning to green when the battery is fully charged. A red light tells you that the battery is running very low and you should charge it soon.

Replacement Chargers

If you misplace or damage your power adapter, you can replace it, by ordering from one of several vendors and community groups here at http://wiki.laptop.org/go/Spare_parts.

Any charger whose jack fits into the XO's power port and outputs the right polarity between 11 and 18 volts will work. For a thorough discussion of electrical specifications and evolving battery updates, read http://wiki.laptop.org/go/Battery_and_power.

Charging using a solar panel or car adapter

With the right connector that fits your XO's DC input on the left side of the XO, you can connect to alternative power peripherals such as a solar panel, foot pedal, or car adapter to charge your XO battery.  The specifications above should drive your decision. Several specific options are available at http://wiki.laptop.org/go/Product_News.

Troubleshooting

If your battery charge gets extremely low during shipping, your XO may not start up. Plugging in external power may not charge the battery.

The solution is to remove the battery, power up the laptop on external power and then insert the battery. Then it will charge and the charging light will be on.

If you're the tinkering type, several more advanced battery troubleshooting tips are at: http://wiki.laptop.org/go/XO_Troubleshooting_Battery.
